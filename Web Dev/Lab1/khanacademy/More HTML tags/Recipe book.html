Plov

  • Time: 1 hr 45 mins
  • Serves: 10 servings
Ingredients Quantity
avocado oil or canola oil ½ cup
beef chuck 3 lbs
small onion 1
large carrots 6
cumin 3 tablespoons
turmeric 1 teaspoon
kosher salt 3 tablespoons
hot water 6 cups
grain rice 3 cups
kosher salt 3 tablespoons

Step 1:Before you do anything, rinse the rice until the water is clear. Then add water into the bowl and let it sit while you prep the rest of the ingredients..

Step 2:Prepare the carrots by cutting them on an angle and then into thicker matchsticks, about ½ inch thick.

Step 3:Cut the onions into a small dice.

Step 4:Add 6 cups of hot water. Note: The goal is for the water to be a bit saltier than you think you need, the rice will absorb a lot of the salt and will help to season it. This is when you taste and see if you’d like any additional seasoning.

Step 5:Add a whole garlic head into the rice, make sure to tuck it in. Note: Peel back most of the layers until you only have 1-2 layers left. Make sure to clean off any of the dirt on the bottom and you can cut off the top a bit or leave it whole. You can clean the garlic to peel easier underwater.

Besbarmaq

  • Time: 3 hours 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4 servings
Ingredients Quantity
beef with bone 2 lbs
large onion 1
bay leaf 1
ground pepper 1
Water to cover 2 liter
plain flour 600 grams

Step 1:Put your meat and 1/2 the onion in a deep enough dish to cover it with cold water leaving enough space on the top so that it doesn't boil over.

Step 2:Let it cook for about 2 1/2 hours or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

Step 3:Sprinkle the work top with sifted flour and divide your dough into a few small balls

Step 4:Now cook the noodles in batches in the same stock for about 7-8 minutes. Sieve them out on to the big plate leaving the space in the center for meat. Then add your meat chopped in bite size pieces and put it in the center of the dish. Pour some broth over the meat.

Step 5:Strain the broth and serve in bowls as shorpa alongside the platter of Beshbarmak
